Walleye

  • Walleye are a freshwater fish native to North America.
  • They are known for their excellent vision in low light conditions.
  • Walleye are a popular game fish and are highly prized for their taste.
  • They can grow up to 31 inches in length and weigh up to 20 pounds.
  • Walleye are typically found in lakes and rivers with clear, cool water.

Salmon

  • Salmon are a type of fish found in both the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
  • They are known for their long migrations to spawn in freshwater streams.
  • Salmon are an important food source for humans and wildlife.
  • They can grow up to 58 inches in length and weigh up to 126 pounds.
  • Salmon are known for their pink flesh, which is rich in omega-3 fatty acids.

Panfish

  • Panfish are a group of small, edible freshwater fish.
  • They are popular with anglers due to their abundance and ease of catching.
  • Panfish include species such as bluegill, crappie, and sunfish.
  • They are typically found in lakes, ponds, and slow-moving rivers.
  • Panfish are often cooked whole and are a popular choice for fish fries.

Pike

  • Pike are a predatory fish found in freshwater lakes and rivers.
  • They are known for their aggressive feeding behavior and sharp teeth.
  • Pike are popular with anglers due to their size and fighting ability.
  • They can grow up to 59 inches in length and weigh up to 55 pounds.
  • Pike are often caught using artificial lures and live bait.
