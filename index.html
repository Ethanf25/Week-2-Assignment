My Favorite Michigan Waters to Fish

The Detroit River

Is a 32-mile-long river that flows from Lake St. Clair to Lake Erie. It is a popular fishing spot for walleye, bass, and perch. It is also home to an elusive fish called the lake sturgeon, these fish are highly protected by the Michigan DNR and are commonly caught by accident when fishing for other native species.


Lake St. Clair

Is a freshwater lake that lies between Lake Huron and Lake Erie. It is known for its excellent fishing opportunities, including walleye, muskie, and smallmouth bass. It is also a popular spot for boating, swimming, and other water activities.

Lake Michigan

Is one of the five Great Lakes of North America and the only one located entirely within the United States. It is known for its beautiful beaches, clear waters, and excellent fishing opportunities. Some of the most popular fish to catch in Lake Michigan include salmon, trout, and perch.

Northern Michigan Rivers

Are home to some of the best salmon fishing in the state. The Au Sable River, Manistee River, and Pere Marquette River are just a few of the many rivers that offer excellent fishing opportunities. These rivers are also popular for canoeing, kayaking, and other outdoor activities.
